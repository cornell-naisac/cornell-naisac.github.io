Taughannock Falls Hiking Trip

To start the semester NAISAC embarked on a number of community building activities. We went on a hiking trip on September 16th to Taughannock Falls in Ithaca, and hiked the 2 mile trail to the base of the waterfall. Afterwards, we had a swim day on Cayuga Lake and celebrated the end of summer!

Sundance Native Film Series At Turning Stone

On Saturday October 13th NAISAC made a trip to the Turning Stone Casino and Resort to watch a short film series by various Native filmmakers affiliated with the Sundance Film Company. Film directors included Bethany Yellowtail, and Shaandin Tome.

NAISAC Pumpkin Carving Workshop

On October 16th NAISAC and the Akwe:kon program house co-hosted a Pumpkin Carving Night in the Community Room! We watched “A Nightmare Before Christmas”, and painted and carved pumpkins of all sizes!

Akwesasne Freedom School Visit

On October 23rd thanks to a cultural exchange program between Lehman Alternative School here in Ithaca, and the Akwesasne Freedom School in the Akwesasne Mohawk Territory, a number of students from the Freedom School were able to visit Cornell’s campus for an evening of food and fun! They first attended the weekly Faculty Fellows Dinner in the Robert Purcell Eatery, and then received a tour of Akwe:kon followed by a paint night in the Community Room!

Canandaigua Treaty Day

The Canandaigua Treaty brought about peace between the Six Nations (Seneca, Cayuga, Onondaga, Oneida, Mohawk, and Tuscarora) and the U.S., and was negotiated and signed by sachems representing the Grand Council of the Six Nations and Colonel Timothy Pickering, the official agent of George Washington on November 11th, 1794. It also recognized the sovereignty of the Six Nations to govern and set laws as individual nations. This yearly commemoration is a time of rededication of the agreement, helping to ensure that the "chain of friendship" and agreements between nations remain current and vibrant. NAISAC attended this event and took part in the march to the courthouse in Canandaigua. Volunteer members helped scrape squash and prepare food for the dinner as well.

Culture Fest

Join the NAISAC at the Culture Fest - an annual event celebrating diversity among cultures at Cornell. NAISAC took part in the first Culture Fest last year, and invited a group of Onondaga dancers and singers to campus for the event. NAISAC showcased cultural items such as black ash splint baskets, and paintings by various Indigenous artists. For those that join Cornell in the spring, this is a great opportunity to find out more about our community!
